Geimon!

Geimon! (Pronounced: Game On!) is an Online-Only, Two-Player Competitive Card Game.

From a worldspace teeming with life, cowboys, wizards, scientists, and knights all meet and clash within Geimon! Science is on the rise, but kings and castles, cowboys and outlaws, all still roam the wilds; and deep below, hidden in the histories lies a dark, ancient magic threatening to make a return.

 When creating Geimon!, we had three key goals:

  1) To make a card game where every time you queue up, you never know what’s going to happen. We would do everything in our power to prevent samey deck lists and optimized combos.
  2) To make a game that’s actually a game. To prevent endless negations, frustrating automatic destruction, infinite recovery, and convoluted text. To make each card balanced against every other, to make it so that     nearly every game goes right down to the line, and every draw matters.
  3) To give each card and related cards distinct personalities so that when you play them, you would say: “Oh yeah, that’s totally something he would do.”

 We wanted to make a game where even a deck of random cards has a fighting chance.


'Best of 1' and 'Best of 3' games are offered.

How You Win:
• Reduce your Opponent’s life to 0. (Starting Life is 20)
• Your Opponent would draw a card(s), but their Deck is empty.

Each Player designs their own Deck to play, or chooses a Starter option.

  Decks must be between 60 and 100 cards. There is nothing else to worry about, no side cards or extra zones. And even simpler, every single card is limited to 1 copy in a Deck, so every single card you see will be unique.

At the start of every game, a Player picks up the top 5 cards of their Deck.

  This is your Starting Hand. If you don't like your Starting Hand, then you can optionally redraw once by placing your Starting Hand on the bottom of your Deck and picking up the next 5 cards. You must keep this new Hand.

After redraws have been decided, a Totem is chosen.

  For the first game of a match, or the only game in a best of 1, a random Totem is rolled. Totems are a modification to the game's mechanics. The loser of the previous game in a match chooses the Totem for the next game from a shortlist of 3 randomly selected options, different to the one seen in previous games.






 
On a Player's turn, there are 6 ‘Phases’: Intermission, Draw, Main 1, Battle, Main 2, and End.

  On the first Player's turn, Battle and Main 2 are skipped.

The Intermission Phase is used to handle maintenance costs, as well as pre-draw interactions.

During the Draw Phase, even on the first turn, the Player whose turn it is draws 1 card.

In Main Phase 1, the turn Player can activate Action, Equipment, and Obelisk cards, Rally Champions, or set Rush and Reflex cards face-down for later.

  They can also place Champions and Obelisks into their Reserve from their Hand, or attach Equipments from their Hands to such cards in their Reserve. Doing so has no effect on the game other than revealing the cards, clearing their hand, or readying for later plays. As explained in the section on the Reserve, it acts mostly as a bench, where Players can construct plays in view, but unable to be stopped directly, just planned against by their Opponents.

In the Battle Phase, you declare one at a time which of your Champions are attacking this Phase. Champions can attack the turn they are Rallied, unless something stops them.

  When you declare an attack, your Opponent can declare a Blocker, i.e. which Champion they are placing in front of your attack. Champions blocking take the attacking Champion's Damage. If they are reduced to 0 Life, they are Destroyed, and go to the Tomb. Blocking Champions deal their Damage to their attackers. Each Champion can only block once per Battle Phase.

The order of a battle goes as follows: Attack Declaration, Blocker Declaration, Flip Resolution, Damage Calculation.
To break it down better:

  1) An attack is declared by Player 1. If they have Priority on the Champion, they must declare an attack Target, or simply that they are attacking directly.
    1b) Their Opponent, Player 2, could, if they have one, respond with a Rush card or a Reflex card or effect to that declaration.

  2) If Player 1 was attacking directly, Player 2 can declare a blocker, or they could not.
   2b) If no card is used in response to the attack declaration, and no blocker is declared, then the attack goes through direct and skips to Damage Calculation, where that Champion's Damage is deducted from the Opponent's Life.

  3) If a face-down blocker was declared, then before conducting Damage Calculation that Champion is flipped face-up and any Flip effects are activated. Then, a Path begins, and resolves depending on if there are any additional Steps either Player wishes to take.

  4) Then, Damage Calculation is performed between the two battling Champions.

  Damage Thresholds may have to be met in order to Damage certain Champions, but they do not reduce the Damage taken if they are met or exceeded. Each attack is separate and Champions cannot team up to overcome a Damage Threshold.

  Leftover Battle Damage over a Champion's Life is dealt to your Opponent. Unless stated otherwise, Champions can only attack once each during a Battle Phase. Only Battle Damage overextends to hit the Player controlling that card, targeted or effect damage to a Champion does not roll over to hit a Player. It stops when the Damage runs out, or the Champion is Destroyed. If a Champion would be Destroyed by battle, and the destruction is negated, the Champion is instead reduced to 1 Life.

In Main Phase 2, a Player can do all the same things they did in Main 1.

And in the End Phase, certain effects will resolve, and like in the Intermission, Players can make skillful plays before the start of the next turn.

  At the end of your End Phase, if you have 7 or more cards in your Hand, you must discard cards so that you have 6 cards in your Hand.






 
The Types of Cards:

Unless stated otherwise, only the turn Player can activate cards, and only during their main Phases.

  Each Player can only use each effect of a card once per turn; if a Champion is Rallied, you use its effect, and then it is Destroyed, but you Resurrect it, you cannot use its effect again (that turn). Every effect is a hard once-per-turn. Every effect is also Optional unless it is expressly stated to be Mandatory. Mandatory effects are the only effects in the game that can be used more than once per turn, as they must ALWAYS trigger when given the opportunity to.

• Champion: Cards that are placed onto the Zone in the Champion Area and symbolize creatures. They are the primary actors in battle.

You can only Rally 1 Champion with a Rally Cost of Basic per turn, this is referred to as a Basic Rally. Basic Champions can be Rallied face-up or face-down in the Champion Area. Basic Champions with conditions cannot be rallied facedown, a condition will be clearly stated above the cost line.


Starting the turn after you set a Champion face-down, you can flip them face-up during one of your Main Phases. A Champion flipped face-down by a card effect cannot be Flip Rallied on the same turn. Champions are also flipped face-up before Damage Calculation (if they are attacked, or used as a blocker). You can rally as many Elite Champions (Champions with costs other than Basic) as possible per turn, as many as you can pay for, should you desire. These Elite Champions can only be rallied face-up.

You will notice that on a Champion card it has a few important things to take note of (From top to bottom):

1) Tags: These are 'Cryptbound' and 'Undead' on this card, and these are used to anchor a card into an archetype and are referenced on support cards. For instance, even on this card, it says Retrieve 1 Other Undead when it enters the Tomb. That effect searches for all Champions with the Tag: Undead, and allows you to interact with them.

2) Passives: These are effects like 'Deathward', and they apply unique effects to the cards they're on. Most of the time, these effects do not activate, they simple apply to the card or around the card. You can hover your mouse over a card's passives to read what they do.

3) Effects: These effects trigger at specific times and are listed on a card after its passives. This card only has 1 effect, and that is when it is sent to the Tomb, from anywhere, you can optionally activate the effect to search through your Tomb and retrieve any 1 Undead other than this card.

4) Damage / Life / Damage Threshold: In order on the bottom of a Champion are its Damage (in red), Life (in blue), and Damage Threshold (in lighter blue). These are explained in more depth in the 'What's In A Turn?' section above in the 'Battle Phase' section.

Importantly, there are a few new things not seen on the Skeleton Guard before:

1) Condition: A card's condition must always be met before it can be rallied, very few cards bypass a card's condition.

2) Cost: A card's cost must be paid before it can be normally activated, there are quite a few effects in the game that bypass a card's cost.

3) Declarable Effects: Some effects on a card are declarable effects, to use a declarable effect, you first meet its condition (the part before a colon ':', not counting the name of the effect, the colon after that), then you pay its cost (the part of the text before a semicolon ';'), then your Opponent is given a chance to respond, and then finally when you wind back to this card's effect on the Path (described elsewhere in the rules) you resolve the declared effect.

4) Exhaustion: Exhaustion effects can only be used if the card has not already attacked or used an Exhaustion effect. Exhaustion effects mean that for the rest of the turn that card can no longer use other Exhaustion effects or declare attacks.


• Action: Cards that are played on the Zone briefly within the Arsenal Area, but once their effects resolve they go to the Tomb.

An Example Action card.

These cards can also have conditions, costs, and then you resolve their effect before they go to the Tomb.


• Equipment: Cards that must be kept on the Zone within the Arsenal Area and are attached to a face-up Champion. If the Champion leaves the Champion Area, even if they are flipped face-down, their Equipment goes to the Tomb.

An Example Equipment card.

Can only be played when there is a Champion on the Zone, you must discard 1 to activate, then you Attach, yes unless it says otherwise, you can attach an Equipment to a face-up Champion on either Player's side.


• Obelisk: Cards that are played onto the Zone within the Arsenal Area and remain there until destroyed.

An Example Obelisk card.

After paying the cost, this card remains on the Zone until removed. Its effect is passive and must be addressed in all instances of a Champion attempting to attack (for this example).

An Example Commander Obelisk card.

As hovering over Mount describes, the subsequent 'Mounted' effects cannot be used unless this card has a Commander. But, when it does have one, then once per turn you can use each of a Commander Obelisk's effects.

Commander Obelisks are special, when they have a Commander (are mounted), and that Commander leaves the Zone and goes specifically to the Tomb or Void, then the Commander Obelisk is Destroyed and is sent to the Tomb. Other removal, such as the Commander going to the Hand, Reserve, or being set face-down, does not Destroy the Mounted Commander Obelisk, instead it simply frees it up to be mounted again.


• Rush: Cards that can be played at Reflex speed in response to others by the turn Player from within their Hand. These cards go into the Arsenal Area before going to the Tomb. The turn Player can place these cards face-down in the Arsenal Area to activate them in response to an Opponent's actions on their turn. Once a card is placed face-down, it cannot be used until the Intermission Phase of the next turn.

An Example Rush card.


• Reflex: Cards that must be placed face-down before they can be activated. They are mainly used to respond to an Opponent's actions, as they can be stepped after them on the Path. Once a card is placed face-down, it cannot be used until the Intermission Phase of the next turn.

An Example Reflex card.



Before a card or effect resolves (is played), it goes on The Path.

  When a card or effect is activated, it Steps onto the Path, either taking the first Step (Path Step 1), or following another in the ongoing Path (Path Step 2 or greater). Rush and Reflex effects are the only effects that can respond to an ongoing Path. If two effects reach triggers at the same time, such as when you Mill 2 and the two cards have "If Sent to Tomb" effects, then you can decide the order of those effects on the Path. Your Opponent can respond after every action you take. Rallies (face-up or face-down) and setting cards face-down in your Arsenal do not start Paths inherently, but they can still be responded to by effects that take the first Step onto the Path. Only card activations and effect activations start Paths.

  When a Path begins to resolve (reaches the end with no further steps to take), it resolves backwards, taking the last Step first (resolving the last effect placed onto the Path first), in a first-in/last-out style. Cards and effects cannot respond to things on the Path whilst it is mid-resolution. They must be Stepped one after another whilst building the Path, and then left to play out as they will. As such, cards can theoretically be activated in an order to play around specific responses.

  Example 1: A Champion declares an attack (Not starting a Path). Then, as Path Step 1, the Opposing Player activates one of their face-down cards, a Rush card, to respond to their declaration of an attack. Then, as Path Step 2, the attacking Player responds with a Reflex of their own to negate the defending Player's Rush, for this example look up to "Parry!". The Path resolves backwards, the negation triggers at Step 2, then when reaching Step 1, the card has been negated, has no effect, and the attack goes through.

  Example 2: A Champion is rallied (Not starting a Path), but it declares its On Rally effect to Add 1 Equipment card (as Path Step 1). Then, the opposing Player responds with a Reflex effect of their Champion (as Path Step 2) to Discard 1 for cost and Destroy 1 card on the Zone for effect. The Path resolves backwards, with the Destroyed card being the freshly Rallied Champion, but because the effect was not negated, when the Path reaches Step 1, the Player who rallied still gets to search their Deck for 1 Equipment and add it to their Hand.



The Zone:



The board is referred to as the Zone, and is split into various areas: Champion, Arsenal, Reserve, Tomb, and Void.

  When cards and effects refer to interacting with cards "On the Zone", the exact definition is: Cards within the Champion Area and The Arsenal. Cards in the Reserve, Tomb, Void, and each others' Decks and Hands are not considered "On the Zone".

  When counting a Player's cards currently controlled, look only to the total sum of cards in their Champions Area and their Arsenal (face-up and face-down).

The Champion Area is where Champions go after they are Rallied. The Arsenal Area is where nearly all other cards go after they are activated.

The Reserve is where cards can come into play without entering the Zone.

  Champions and Obelisks in the Reserve can be activated as if they were in the Players Hand. So, during an appropriate time, which is usually just during that Player's Main Phase, they can (if the card's conditions are met) pay their costs and play them from the Reserve onto the field like normal. The only exception is that Basic Champions cannot be Rallied from the Reserve face-down, unless an effect declares otherwise.

  Cards that go into the Reserve are not counted as played, and in most instances cannot be negated. But, for example, when a Champion leaves the Reserve to enter the Zone, then it is Rallied, and it can be negated.

  When a card is played from the Reserve, it and any attached or linked cards are also played, such as a Champion or Obelisk with an Equipment, or a Champion mounted onto a Commander Obelisk. At such time, the costs for all cards must be paid at once, and you cannot activate any of the cards linked together if you cannot pay all of their costs at the same time.

The Tomb is where cards go when they are used, or Destroyed.

The Void is where cards go when they are Obliterated.

  Most of the time cards in the Void cannot be interacted with or Reclaimed.






 
• Add: Select a card(s) from your Deck and place it into your Hand.

• Blink: Rally from your Hand (ignoring cost). You must still meet conditions.

• Bottom Stack: Place a designated card on the bottom of the Deck.

• Bounce: Return a card on the Zone to the Hand.

• Bury: Send from the Deck to the Tomb.

• Cards Controlled: The total number of cards between a Player's Champions and cards in their Arsenal.

• Cost: Listed on a card before its effect and must be paid before it goes onto the Path. Responses to cards come after their Cost is paid, and are placed after it on the Path.

• Deploy: Rally from your Reserve (ignoring cost). You must still meet conditions.

• Destroy: Send the designated card to the Tomb.

• Discard: Send from the Hand to the Tomb. You choose the card(s) unless stated otherwise.

• Excavate: Reveal cards from the top of the Deck. If not specified, returned cards are shuffled back into the Deck.

• Exhaustion: Exhaustion effects can only be used if the card has not already attacked or used an Exhaustion effect. Exhaustion effects mean that for the rest of the turn that card can no longer use other Exhaustion effects or declare attacks.

• Flip: Champions that are face-down activate this effect when they are turned face-up (either by Flip Rally or during Battle).

• Mill: Send from the top of the Deck to the Tomb. Cards that are Milled also count as if they were Buried.

• Obliterate: Send the designated card to the Void.

• Offer: Reduce your Life by an amount listed, usually as the cost for another effect.

• Rally: Place a Champion into your Champion Area (most commonly from the Hand or Reserve), by meeting its conditions and paying its cost. After activation, a Champion card remains active on the Zone.

• Reclaim: Take from the Void into your Hand.

• Recruit: Rally from your Deck (ignoring cost). You must still meet conditions.

• Resurrect: Rally from your Tomb (ignoring cost). You must still meet conditions.

• Retrieve: Take from the Tomb into your Hand.

• Sacrifice: Send a number of your Rallied Champions to the Tomb, usually as a cost.

• Spin: Shuffle card(s) into the Deck from a designated location.

• Stack: Place a designated card on the top of the Deck.

• Target: Select a card to be the recipient of an effect.

• Unleash: Rally from your Void (ignoring cost). You must still meet conditions.
